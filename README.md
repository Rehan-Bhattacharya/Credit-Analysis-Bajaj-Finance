# Credit Analysis — Bajaj Finance Limited
Independent credit research prepared by Rehan Bhattacharya | CFA Level 2 Candidate

## Overview

A professional one-page credit summary of Bajaj Finance Limited (NSE: BAJFINANCE), India's largest NBFC by AUM. This report analyses the company's financial performance, asset quality, capital adequacy, and credit risk profile across FY2023–FY2025, and arrives at an independent credit opinion and suggested rating.
Data sources: FY2025 Annual Report, Q4 FY2025 Investor Deck, CRISIL and India Ratings public disclosures, RBI regulatory filings.

## What This Report Covers

- Business overview and lending verticals across B2C, SME, commercial, rural, mortgages, and payments segments
- Three-year financial performance tracking across AUM growth, NII, PAT, and credit cost
- Asset quality analysis — Gross NPA, Net NPA, and provisioning trends across FY2023–FY2025
- Capital adequacy assessment — CRAR, Tier 1 Capital Ratio relative to RBI regulatory floor
- Profitability metrics — NIM (on avg. AUM), ROA, ROE, and credit cost trends
- Risk assessment across four key risk dimensions with specific mitigants identified
- Cross-reference of domestic ratings (CRISIL AAA/Stable) against international agency assessments (S&P BBB-/Positive, Moody's
  Baa3/Stable)
- Independent credit opinion with defined deterioration triggers

## Key Results

- AUM Growth
 AUM expanded from ₹2,47,379 Cr (FY2023) to ₹4,16,661 Cr (FY2025) — a 26% YoY growth in FY2025 — reflecting continued franchise
 expansion across 4,263 branches and 101.82 million customers. The hub-and-spoke rural model and 70.6M-user Bajaj Finserv app provide
 structural distribution advantages.

- Asset Quality
  Gross NPA of 0.96% and Net NPA of 0.44% in FY2025 remain well below RBI's Upper Layer NBFC benchmarks, despite a 12 bps deterioration   in GNPA from FY2024 levels. The spike in loan losses and provisions to ₹7,966 Cr in FY2025 (from ₹4,631 Cr in FY2024) signals
  elevated stress in unsecured consumer and SME segments — the primary credit concern near-term.

- Capital Adequacy
  CRAR stood at 21.93% in FY2025, materially above the 15% RBI regulatory floor. Tier 1 Capital Ratio of 21.09% reflects a well-
  capitalised balance sheet. A ~₹1B ECB raised (fully hedged) and a deposit target of 25% of borrowings support future capital access
  at competitive rates.

- Profitability
  ROA of 4.57% and ROE of 19.19% in FY2025 reflect solid but moderating returns — NIM compressed 49 bps in FY2025 driven by rising cost   of funds (7.97%) and competitive pressure from fintechs and banks in consumer lending. Credit cost rose to ~2.2% of avg. AUM, the
  highest in the three-year window analysed.

- Credit Opinion
  Suggested rating: AAA / Stable (NBFC Issuer — INR borrowings). Outlook: Stable — credit profile intact; improving trajectory expected   in FY2026 as ECL provisioning normalises. Deterioration watch triggers: sustained unsecured NPA worsening beyond GNPA 1.5%, CRAR
  dropping below 18%, or a second round of regulatory sanction.
